
    19 Events in Honor of the 19th Amendment to be Held this Weekend Across Iowa
DES MOINES, IA – Iowans for Hillary will celebrate Women's Equality Day by holding 19 events in honor the of 19th Amendment, a cornerstone of the Women's Equality Movement which gave every woman in America the right to vote. When the amendment was signed into law 87 years ago, Americans needed leaders with the strength, leadership and political will to fight for equality, and Iowans for Hillary has assembled a few of this generation's leaders to celebrate this important weekend.
"This Women's Equality Day effort not only commemorates women finally being ensured the right to vote, but also celebrates the women who have broken barriers and shattered stereotypes," said Senator Hillary Clinton. "When I meet young women across the country, I am encouraged by their enthusiasm and am reminded that we need to continue the fight to protect and defend women's rights."
Hillary Clinton supporters who will be visiting these events over the weekend are:

Marti Anderson – Member of the Women's Leadership Council and Victims Advocate
Bonnie Campbell – Iowa Midwest Co-Chair and former Director of the Office of Violence Against Women
Dr. Andy McGuire – Iowans for Hillary State Campaign Co-Chair
Dolores Huerta – Longtime labor and civil rights leader and co-founder of the United Farm Workers Union
Lieutenant General Claudia Kennedy – First woman to be appointed to the rank of Lieutenant General in the U.S. Armed Services
Christie Vilsack – Former First Lady of Iowa
