
    The President today announced the appointment of Thomas E. Morgan as Chairman of the U.S. Section of the Permanent Joint Board on Defense-United States and Canada. Morgan, of Fredericktown, Pa., replaces Charles S. Gubser, resigned.
Morgan was born October 13, 1906, in Ellsworth, Pa. He received an M.D. from Wayne University in 1934.
Morgan practiced medicine and surgery in Fredericktown from 1934 to 1973. In 1944 he was elected to the 79th Congress, and he was reelected to each succeeding Congress until his retirement in January 1977.
