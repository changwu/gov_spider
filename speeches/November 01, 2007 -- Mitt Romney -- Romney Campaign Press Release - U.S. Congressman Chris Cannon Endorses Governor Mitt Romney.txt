Congressman Cannon joins a Congressional Whip Team led by Congressional Liaison Representative Jim McCrery (R-LA) and Co-House Congressional Liaisons Representatives Dave Camp (R-MI) and Howard "Buck" McKeon (R-CA).
Background On U.S. Congressman Chris Cannon:
U.S. Congressman Chris Cannon Was Elected To Congress On November 5, 1996. In January 2003, Congressman Cannon was named Chairman of the House Judiciary Subcommittee on Commercial and Administrative Law. As Chairman, he was involved in legislation regarding bankruptcy reform, privacy, interstate compacts and tort reform. He is currently the subcommittee's Ranking Member. He also serves on the House Oversight and Government Reform Committee and House Natural Resources Committee. In January 2003, he was elected chairman of the Western Caucus, a group of more than 50 Congressmen working on rational, balanced and sound resource management.
Before his election to Congress, Congressman Cannon was involved in the steel industry. In 1987, he and his brother purchased and reopened Geneva Steel in Orem, UT, restoring 2,500 steel worker jobs. In 1990, he purchased Geneva's new venture division, now called Cannon Industries. He also served in a number of political positions including as Utah Finance Chairman for President George Bush's reelection campaign in 1992. He has also served as the Utah Republican Party's Finance Chairman and as a delegate to the Republican National Conventions in 1992 and 1996. He and his wife, Claudia, have eight children and reside in Mapleton, UT.
