
    The President today announced his intention to nominate Rosemary M. Collyer to be General Counsel of the National Labor Relations Board for a term of 4 years. She would succeed William A. Lubbers.
Mrs. Collyer is currently Chairman of the Federal Mine Safety and Health Review Commission. Previously, she was senior associate with the firm of Sherman & Howard in Denver, CO (1977-1981); instructor, Personnel Prediction and Research, Inc. (1973-1974); creative director, Pennington and Richard Associates (1972-1973); director of public relations, Manlius Pebble Hill School (1971-1972); and reporter with the Canadian Register, Toronto (1969-1970).
Mrs. Collyer graduated from Trinity College (B.A., 1968) and the University of Denver College of Law (J.D., 1977). She is married, has one child, and resides in Chevy Chase, MD. She was born November 19, 1945, in Port Chester, NY.
