In Town Pool Report #8 - Departure and Comments
President Biden exited the White House at 5:49 PM.
He made some comments on Tyre Nichols. Some quotes below, please check against transcript.
He told reporters he spoke with Tyre Nichols' "mother and expressed my condolences." He said he would work with Congress to pass the George Floyd act and said he was "pleased she called for peaceful protest."
Biden said "I'm obviously very concerned" about violence but said Nichols' mother "has made a very strong plea."
He said "I told her that I have some Idea of what that loss is like."

In Town Pool Report #10 - More Biden Quotes
Below are some more quotes from the President's comments about Tyre Nichols. Again check against the official transcript:
"I spoke with Tyre's mother and expressed my condolences and told her that I was going to be making the case to the Congress to pass the George Floyd Act. We should get this under control. I can only do so much on the executive order at the federal level."
"I was really pleased that she called for peaceful protest, no violence."
He said they spoke for 10 or 15 minutes.
On the potential for violence:
"I'm obviously very concerned about it. But I think she has made a very strong plea. She's obviously in enormous pain."
"I told her I had some idea of what that loss is like and although it is impossible to believe now, a time will come when his memory brings a smile before a tear."
What is at stake:
"First of all innocent people's lives, number one. Number two it has a lot to say and do with the image of America. It has a lot to do with whether or not we are the country we say we are."
