
    The President today announced his intention to appoint T. Kenneth Cribb, Jr., to be a member of the Council of the Administrative Conference of the United States for a term of 3 years. This is a reappointment.
Since 1985 Mr. Cribb has been Counselor to the Attorney General. Previously, he was a member of the White House staff as Assistant Counselor to the President, 1982-1985; Assistant Director of the Office of Cabinet Affairs and Staff Assistant to the President, 1981-1982; Deputy Director of the legal and administrative agencies group during the 1980 transition, Office of the President-elect; and national director of the Intercollegiate Studies Institute from 1971 to 1977.
Mr. Cribb graduated from Washington & Lee University (B.A., 1970) and the University of Virginia (J.D., 1980). He resides in Arlington, VA. Mr. Cribb was born August 7, 1948, in Spartanburg, SC.
