Background On Dr. Pedro Celis, Ph.D.
Pedro Celis Is A Distinguished Engineer With Microsoft Corporation, Which He Joined In 1998. In 2003, Celis was nominated by President Bush to serve on the President's Information Technology Advisory Committee (PITAC), a group of information infrastructure experts that advise the president on how to maintain U.S. pre-eminence in information technology. Celis was born and raised in Monterrey, Mexico and holds an engineering degree from the Monterrey Institute of Technology, as well as Master's and Ph.D. degrees in computer science from the University of Waterloo in Canada. He currently holds 15 U.S. patents. In addition to his professional accomplishments, Dr. Celis has served as a board member of the Washington State Hispanic Chamber of Commerce and in 2001 founded the Washington Chapter of the Republican National Hispanic Assembly and became its first chairman. From 2005-2007, he served as National Chairman of the Republican National Hispanic Assembly.
