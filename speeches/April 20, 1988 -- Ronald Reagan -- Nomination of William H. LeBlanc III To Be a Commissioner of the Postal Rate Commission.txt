
    The President today announced his intention to nominate William H. LeBlanc III to be a Commissioner of the Postal Hate Commission for the term expiring November 22, 1994. This is a reappointment.
Since 1987 Mr. LeBlanc has been Commissioner of the United States Postal Rate Commission in Washington, DC. Prior to this, he was president and general manager of Baton gouge Supply Company, Inc., 1980-1987, and a general manager working with management teams of six family-owned businesses, 1972-1980.
Mr. LeBlanc graduated from Louisiana State University (B.S., 1972). He was born May 15, 1948, in Baton Rouge, LA. He served in the United States Marine Corps, 1967-1970. He is married, has three children, and resides in Silver Spring, MD.
