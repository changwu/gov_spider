
    On Wednesday, October 30, 2002, the President signed into law:
H.R. 669, which designates the facility of the United States Postal Service located at 127 Social Street in Woonsocket, Rhode Island, as the Alphonse F. Auclair Post Office Building;
H.R. 670, which designates the facility of the United States Postal Service located at 7 Commercial Street in Newport, Rhode Island, as the Bruce F. Cotta Post Office Building;
H.R. 3034, which redesignates the facility of the United States Postal Service located at 89 River Street in Hoboken, New Jersey, as the Frank Sinatra Post Office Building;
H.R. 3738, which designates the facility of the United States Postal Service located at 1299 North 7th Street in Philadelphia, Pennsylvania, as the Herbert Arlene Post Office Building;
H.R. 3739, which designates the facility of the United States Postal Service located at 6150 North Broad Street in Philadelphia, Pennsylvania, as the Rev. Leon Sullivan Post Office Building;
H.R. 3740, which designates the facility of the United States Postal Service located at 925 Dickinson Street in Philadelphia, Pennsylvania, as the William A. Cibotti Post Office Building;
H.R. 4102, which designates the facility of the United States Postal Service located at 120 North Maine Street in Fallon, Nevada, as the "Rollan D. Melton Post Office Building";
H.R. 4717, which designates the facility of the United States Postal Service located at 1199 Pasadena Boulevard in Pasadena, Texas, as the Jim Fonteno Post Office Building;
H.R. 4755, which designates the facility of the United States Postal Service located at 204 South Broad Street in Lancaster, Ohio, as the Clarence Miller Post Office Building;
H.R. 4794, which designates the facility of the United States Postal Service located at 1895 Avenida Del Oro in Oceanside, California, as the Ronald C. Packard Post Office Building;
H.R. 4797, which redesignates the facility of the United States Postal Service located at 265 South Western Avenue, Los Angeles, California, as the Nat King Cole Post Office; and
H.R. 4851, which designates the facility of the United States Postal Service located at 6910 South Yorktown Avenue in Tulsa, Oklahoma, as the Robert Wayne Jenkins Station.
