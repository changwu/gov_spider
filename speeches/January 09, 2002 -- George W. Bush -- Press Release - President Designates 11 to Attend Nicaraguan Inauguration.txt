
    President Bush Announced His Designation of Eleven Individuals to Represent the United States at the Inauguration of the President of the Republic of Nicaragua
President George W. Bush today announced his designation of eleven individuals to represent the United States at the Inauguration of his Excellency Enrique Bolanos Geyer, President of the Republic of Nicaragua on January 10, 2002.
The following individuals will be members of the delegation representing the United States at the Inauguration of his Excellency Enrique Bolanos Geyer, President of the Republic of Nicaragua.
The Honorable Mel Martinez, Secretary of Housing and Urban Development will serve as the leader of the delegation.

The Honorable Oliver Garza, U.S. Ambassador to Nicaragua
The Honorable John Maisto, Special Assistant to the President and Senior Director for Western Hemisphere Affairs at the National Security Council
The Honorable Lino Gutierrez, Acting Assistant Secretary of State for Western Hemisphere Affairs
Dr. Janet Ballantyne, Counselor, U.S. Agency for International Development
Mr. George Prescott Bush
Mr. James C. Kenny, Vice President and Owner of Kenny Construction Company, Wheeling, Illinois
Mr. Earle Irving Mack, Senior Partner of the Mack Company, Fort Lee, New Jersey
Ms. Anna Navarro, Senior Consultant at Black, Kelly, Scruggs and Healy, Washington, D.C.
Mr. Raul Romero, President and CEO of S&B Infrastructure, Ltd., Washington, D.C.
Mrs. Evelyn Dee Potter Rose, Dallas, Texas
