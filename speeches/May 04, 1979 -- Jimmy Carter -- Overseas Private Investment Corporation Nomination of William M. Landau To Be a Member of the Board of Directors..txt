
    The President today announced that he will nominate William M. Landau, of Scarsdale, N.Y., to be a member of the Board of Directors of the Overseas Private Investment Corporation. He will replace Wallace Bennett, who has retired.
Landau is currently a managing partner in the firm of Mann Judd Landau Certified Public Accountants.
He was born September 10, 1926, in New York City. He received a B.S. degree from Lehigh University and an LL.B. degree from New York Law School. He served 2 years in the U.S. Army Infantry, and he joined the firm of Mann Judd Landau Certified Public Accountants (formerly Fred Landau & Co.) in 1951.
Landus is a certified public accountant in the States of New York, Illinois, and California, a member of the American Institute of CPA's, and a member of the New York Bar Association. He is a past member of the committee on cooperation with bankers and the professional ethics committee of the New York State Society of CPA's.
