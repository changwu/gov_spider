
    On Tuesday, August 12, 2008, the President signed into law:
H.R. 2245, which designates the Department of Veterans Affairs outpatient clinic as the Elwood "Bud" Link Department of Veterans Affairs Outpatient Clinic;
H.R. 4210, which designates the facility of the United States Postal Service as the Dock M. Brown Post Office Building;
H.R. 4918, which designates the Department of Veterans Affairs medical center as the Bruce W. Carter Department of Veterans Affairs Medical Center;
H.R. 5477, which designates the facility of the United States Postal Service as the Chi Mui Post Office Building;
H.R. 5483, which designates the facility of the United States Postal Service as the Private First Class David H. Sharrett II Post Office Building;
H.R. 5631, which designates the facility of the United States Postal Service as the Corporal Bradley T. Arms Post Office Building;
H.R. 6061, which designates the facility of the United States Postal Service as the Kenneth James Gray Post Office Building;
H.R. 6085, which designates the facility of the United States Postal Service as the Gerald R. Ford Post Office Building;
H.R. 6150, which designates the facility of the United States Postal Service as the John P. Gallagher Post Office Building;
H.R. 6340, which designates the Federal building and United States courthouse as the Charles L. Brieant, Jr., Federal Building and United States Courthouse;
S. 3294, which extends the authority of the United States Parole Commission for an additional three years, until November 1, 2011; and
S. 3295, which provides for the appointment of administrative patent judges and administrative trademark judges in the U.S. Patent and Trademark Office.
