
    President George W. Bush announced today that he intends to nominate one person, designate one person, and appoint three people to serve in his Administration:
The President intends to nominate Charles E. Dorkey III, of New York, to be a Member of the Advisory Board of the Saint Lawrence Seaway Development Corporation.
The President intends to designate Stanley E. Taylor, of Virginia, to be Chairman of the Commission on Presidential Scholars.
The President intends to appoint Mark B. Murphy, of New Mexico, to be a Member of the Advisory Board of the National Air and Space Museum.
The President intends to appoint Debra Lynn Crisp, of Oregon, to be a Member of the Klamath River Compact Commission (Federal Representative) and upon appointment, designate Chairman.
The President intends to appoint Steven M. Colloton, of Iowa, to be a Member of the Board of Trustees of the James Madison Memorial Fellowship Foundation (Federal Judiciary) for the remainder of a six year term expiring October 4, 2012.
