
    The President today announced his intention to nominate Harold K. Phillips to be a member of the Board of Directors of the Inter-American Foundation for a term expiring September 20, 1994. Upon confirmation, he will be designated Vice Chairman. This is a reappointment.
Since 1985 Mr. Phillips has been president of the Granja Costaricense De Camorones in San Jose, Costa Rica. He is also an assistant to the chancellor and professor of business administration at National University in San Diego, CA.
Mr. Phillips graduated from the University of Redlands (B.S., 1963) and Harvard University (M.B.A., 1967). He was born September 1, 1940, in Glendale, CA. He is married, has two children, and resides in San Clemente, CA.
