
    On Friday, October 13, 2006, the President signed into law:
H.R. 315, which designates the United States courthouse in Jacksonville, Florida, as the John Milton Bryan Simpson United States Courthouse;
H.R. 562, which authorizes the Government of Ukraine to establish a memorial on Federal land in the District of Columbia to honor the victims of the manmade famine that occurred in Ukraine in 1932-1933;
H.R. 1463, which designates a portion of the Federal building located in Alexandria, Virginia, as the Justin W. Williams United States Attorney's Building;
H.R. 1556, which designates a parcel of land located on the site of the Thomas F. Eagleton United States Courthouse in St. Louis, Missouri, as the Clyde S. Cahill Memorial Park;
H.R. 2322, which designates the Federal building located in McAllen, Texas, as the Kika de la Garza Federal Building;
H.R. 4768, which designates the facility of the United States Postal Service in Beaver, Pennsylvania, as the Robert Linn Memorial Post Office Building;
H.R. 4805, which designates the facility of the United States Postal Service in Clinton, Illinois, as the Gene Vance Post Office Building;
H.R. 5026, which designates the Investigations Building of the Food and Drug Administration in San Juan, Puerto Rico, as the Andres Toro Building;
H.R. 5428, which designates the facility of the United States Postal Service in Morris, Illinois, as the Joshua A. Terando Morris Post Office Building;
H.R. 5434, which designates the facility of the United States Postal Service in Chillicothe, Ohio, as the Larry Cox Post Office;
S. 2856, the "Financial Services Regulatory Relief Act of 2006," which reduces the regulatory burden on banks, savings associations, and credit unions;
S. 3661, the "Wright Amendment Reform Act 2006," which modifies restrictions on air transportation to and from Love Field, Texas; and
S. 3728, the "North Korea Nonproliferation Act of 2006," which amends the Iran and Syria Nonproliferation Act to apply to North Korea.
