
    President George W. Bush today announced his intention to appoint six individuals to serve in his administration:
The President intends to appoint Ronald G. Bellamy of Texas, to be a Member if the President's Commission on White House Fellowships.
The President intends to appoint Carlos M. de Cespedes of Florida, to be a Member of the President's Commission on White House Fellowships.
The President intends to appoint Julia A. King of Maryland, to be a Member of the Advisory Council on Historic Preservation (Historic Preservation Expert), for a four-year term beginning June 11, 2003.
The President intends to appoint Timothy Pawlenty of Minnesota, to be a Member of the Advisory Council on Historic Preservation (Governor), for the remainder of a four-year term expiring June 10, 2005.
The President intends to appoint Ann A. Pritlzlaff of Colorado, to be a Member of the Advisory Council on Historic Preservation (Historic Preservation Expert), for a four-year term beginning June 11, 2003.
The President intends to appoint James E. Geringer of Wyoming, to be Representative of the United States to the Western Interstate Nuclear Board.
